### Stay Safe Online: Prevention Checklist for College Students



* &#x20;  Never Share Your UPI PIN: Remember that a UPI PIN is only required when you are sending money, never to receive a payment or refund.



* &#x20; Verify Customer Care Numbers: Only use official apps or verified websites to contact support; avoid relying on random search engine results for helpline numbers.



* &#x20;   Use Strong Passwords: Create unique, complex passwords for your university portal, email, and social media accounts.



* &#x20;   Enable 2FA: Turn on Two-Factor Authentication for all your major accounts (email, GitHub, social media) to add an extra layer of security.



* &#x20;   Beware of Phishing Links: Do not click on unsolicited links in SMS or WhatsApp messages claiming you have won a prize or that a package delivery failed.



* &#x20;   Secure Your Devices: Keep your laptop and phone locked with a password or biometrics, and never leave them unattended on campus.



* &#x20;   Avoid Public Wi-Fi for Banking: Do not perform financial transactions or access sensitive accounts over unsecured public Wi-Fi networks at cafes or libraries.



* &#x20;   Limit Social Media Oversharing: Don't post pictures of your college ID cards, boarding passes, or real-time location tags that could be used for identity theft.



Reporting Channels:

If you encounter cyber fraud, report it immediately to the National Cyber Crime Portal (cybercrime.gov.in) or call the Helpline 1930.
